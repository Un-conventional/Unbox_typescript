Frequently Asked Questions

Unbox? This is UltraBox but with my ideas added on. This also is sort of a testing ground for me to learn code.
Some ideas come from how sound chips work and that and many ideas may seem excessive but pushing limits is fun.
Who is this mod for? This mod is for anyone regardless of skill level, I strongly suggest checking out UltraBox first though.
Why does Unbox exist? It exists because I wanted it to exist. I got tired of asking "what if X existed" or "why can't W, X, Y apply to Z as well".
Why is the b in Unbox not a capitalized B? Why is it not called UnBox or UNBox? Because I said so and I found it weird having it spelled that way.
How does Unbox differ from UltraBox? Unbox aims to add features Unconventional wants to add. The current features include:
+- 24 Pitch Shift Range I felt the range was too small so I increased it. It still works the same as normal Pitch Shift but with more range.
0%-100% Pulse Width Range The fact that 1%-50% just felt limiting. With what I added, 0-100% is possible if you include the decimal offset. There is just so much that is possible with that range increase

    • How do I add custom samples? Since Unbox doesn't have a web server of its own, you'll have to first upload your sample to a CORS-friendly website.
    • The easiest to use are File Garden or Catbox.
    Next, you have to copy the URL of the sound itself. The URL should end with the filetype, such as .wav or .mp3.

    To import it into Unbox, open the Edit tab, scroll to the bottom, and click on "Add Custom Samples..."

    Paste your url and press "Okay." If everything goes smoothly, Unbox will reload and your sample will be available in the preset list. "TypeError: NetworkError when attempting to fetch resource." is not a bug on our end. That is probably you ISP blocking it.
    Does Unbox have an offline version? NO!, Not yet but you can access UltraBox's here.
    You can either download an OS-specific version that has custom sampling support, or the generic HTML version that lacks custom sampling support.

All song data is contained in the URL after the hash (#) mark, and Unbox running inside your browser converts that data into sound waves.
